# Positioning Rules

## Positioning Thesis
Darling MarTech should position itself as the operator who builds, integrates, and improves the systems that drive demand, conversion, follow-up, and revenue clarity.

The site should not imply:
- only enterprise complexity
- only small-business quick wins
- only websites
- only marketing execution

Instead, it should clearly support **two valid buyer entry states** under one systems-first frame.

## The Two Entry States
### 1. “We have a system, but it is broken.”
This buyer already has tools, traffic, teams, or process — but the stack is fragmented or underperforming.

Best site path:
- Problems hub
- proof pages
- diagnostic tools
- roadmap / conversation CTA

### 2. “We do not really have a system yet.”
This buyer often has:
- a basic website
- scattered tools
- a template platform such as Squarespace, Wix, or WordPress
- no meaningful CRM usage
- no integrated follow-up
- no reporting trust

Best site path:
- system foundation positioning
- productized or scoped foundation offers
- simple diagnostics
- strong before/after operating model visuals

## Corrected Anti-Persona Rule
Do not position small firms, smaller law offices, or local operators as automatic bad fits.

That is inaccurate for the real business and weakens the proof story.

Use this rule instead:
- small is not the problem
- lack of internal marketing team is not the problem
- platform simplicity is not the problem
- the problem is a missing or misused system

## Approved Messaging Patterns
Use:
- system architect
- growth system
- system foundation
- connected growth infrastructure
- strategy, systems, and execution
- one accountable operator
- from missing stack to connected stack
- from fragmented stack to usable operating system

For smaller buyers, use:
- first working growth system
- practical infrastructure for lead capture and follow-up
- a system your business can actually run on
- move beyond brochure websites and disconnected tools

## Avoided Messaging Patterns
Avoid:
- only for enterprise teams
- not for small firms
- not for local businesses
- only for companies with a large internal marketing team
- if you are under a revenue threshold, we are not a fit

Those statements should only be used when tied to a specific premium offer, not as a universal positioning rule.

## Offer Framing Rule
When speaking to smaller law firms, local businesses, and new ventures:
- do not lead with custom engineering language
- do not oversell complexity
- do not force them into enterprise framing

Lead with:
- what is breaking now
- what revenue is being lost
- how the new system fixes intake, follow-up, reviews, visibility, or conversion

## Site Copy Rule
The site should repeatedly communicate this idea:

> This is not about company size. It is about whether the system behind your growth actually works.

## Navigation / IA Implication
The site should eventually support a visible split between:
- broken systems
- missing systems

Possible labels:
- “Fix the system”
- “Build the first real system”

This can live in homepage wayfinding, /problems, or future service cluster pages.
