# Proof Storytelling Rules

## Purpose
Keep proof pages proof-first: **process + system change + outcome**, not client-promotion-first.

---

## Proof page job

Answer, quickly and clearly:

1. **What was broken (or missing)?**
2. **What changed in the system?**
3. **What outcome improved (and how do we know)?**
4. **What would someone like this do next?**

---

## Narrative structure (default)

1. **Stakes (1 screen):** short context + the constraint (no backstory essay)
2. **Pre-state:** symptoms and operational reality
3. **Intervention:** the concrete system change (what was built, connected, removed, or re-sequenced)
4. **Outcomes:** scannable, attributable outcomes (numbers only when they have context)
5. **Why it worked:** brief principles / decisions that made the change durable
6. **Next step:** one trust-appropriate CTA + a small bridge to the most relevant service or tool

---

## Language rules

- Prefer **system change** over **client brand**.
- Prefer **observable behavior** over **vibes** (“handoff gaps closed”, “follow-up automated”, “lead loss reduced”).
- Avoid “we helped X become a leader” style brand promotion.
- Do not list every platform/tool used unless it is causally relevant to the outcome.

---

## Relationship surface rules

Proof pages should not become a “related everything” index.

- Related entities must stay subordinate to the story.
- Prefer a single “Next: implementation path” bridge over multiple related blocks.
- If additional exploration is needed, route to `/proof` or the relevant hub.
