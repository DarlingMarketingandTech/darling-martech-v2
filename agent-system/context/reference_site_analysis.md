# Reference Site Analysis (Qualities, Not Clones)

## Target Standard
Darling Martech should feel like a premium diagnostic/product system, not an agency portfolio shell.

## Desired Qualities
- Bold but disciplined visual hierarchy.
- Fewer, stronger sections with high intentionality.
- Premium restraint over decorative overload.
- High interaction clarity: users always know what to do next.
- Richer proof storytelling tied to measurable outcomes.
- Less portfolio-grid energy; more system-diagnosis energy.
- Services framed as operating systems, not task lists.
- Clear buyer-path segmentation by intent and trust stage.
- Strong confidence in typography and spacing rhythm.
- Visuals that support persuasion and comprehension.

## What "Premium" Means Here
- Clear structure before visual flair.
- Consistent layout grammar across pages.
- Deliberate transitions and narrative pacing.
- Evidence integrated into decision moments.
- Distinctive brand posture without trend-chasing aesthetics.

## What to Avoid
- Generic "modern SaaS" look that could belong to any brand.
- Equal visual weight for all sections.
- Dense card grids with weak signal.
- Decorative imagery that does not improve message delivery.
