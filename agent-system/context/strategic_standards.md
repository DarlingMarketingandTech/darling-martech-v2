# Strategic Standards

## Core Positioning Standard
Darling MarTech is not qualified by company size first.

Darling MarTech is qualified by **system state, revenue friction, and implementation need**.

The business is a fit when revenue is being constrained by one or more of the following:
- disconnected tools
- manual follow-up
- weak or missing attribution
- low-converting website structure
- poor visibility despite strong real-world service quality
- no practical operating system behind lead capture, follow-up, nurture, and reporting

This applies to both:
- complex growth-stage organizations with fragmented infrastructure
- smaller operators with no meaningful system in place yet

## Systems Over Isolated Tactics
We do not frame the work as separate marketing tasks.

We frame the work as building or repairing a connected growth system:
- positioning
- website and conversion flow
- CRM and pipeline structure
- lead capture and routing
- automation and follow-up
- reporting and attribution

The standard is not “more activity.”
The standard is “a system that captures, converts, routes, measures, and compounds demand.”

## Corrected Qualification Model
### Segment A — Complex System Clients
Best fit when the organization already has scale, volume, or operational complexity.

Typical indicators:
- multiple channels or stakeholders
- CRM and reporting distrust
- fragmented martech stack
- operational drag between marketing, sales, and delivery
- revenue visibility problems

### Segment B — System-Starved Operators
Best fit when the business is smaller but lacks a real operating system.

Typical indicators:
- website exists but does not support conversion well
- follow-up is manual or inconsistent
- CRM is absent, underused, or misconfigured
- tools were chosen ad hoc and do not work together
- the owner handles too much manually
- leads are lost due to missed calls, weak intake, or poor process

This segment includes:
- small law firms without an internal marketing team
- local service businesses without integrated systems
- smaller healthcare/service operators
- new businesses that need a first working growth stack, not random tools

## Anti-Persona Standard
We do **not** disqualify businesses because they are small.

We disqualify based on mindset and buying behavior.

Not a fit:
- buyers who want isolated tactics without system change
- businesses that only want a prettier site but do not care about conversion or operations
- teams unwilling to adopt process, automation, or reporting discipline
- price-only buyers who reject implementation logic in favor of the cheapest tool
- DIY control buyers who want ownership of every tool decision but no operating system

The anti-persona is not “mom and pop.”
The anti-persona is **non-system behavior**.

## Productized Foundation Path
For smaller operators and new businesses, the right offer is often not custom enterprise engineering first.

It is a structured foundation build:
- site shell
- CRM setup
- lead capture
- booking / intake flow
- follow-up automation
- basic attribution and visibility

This should be described as a **system foundation build** or **growth system foundation**, not as “just a website.”

## Language Standard
Avoid messaging that implies Darling MarTech only works with large companies.

Prefer language such as:
- built for businesses with a system problem
- for teams with fragmented infrastructure or no real system yet
- from first working stack to complex connected growth infrastructure
- strategy, systems, and execution for businesses that need the whole thing to work together
