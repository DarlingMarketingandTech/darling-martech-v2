# Growth System Audit Strategy

## Purpose

Use the Growth System Audit as the default low-trust entry point for visitors who need diagnosis before they are ready for services or a call.

## Role in the trust ladder

- Low trust: run the Growth System Audit
- Mid trust: see similar proof
- High trust: book a diagnostic call

This replaces scattered low-trust CTA logic with one clearer entry path.

## Strategic direction

- Consolidate overlapping individual tools into the Growth System Audit strategy where practical.
- Keep the audit diagnostic-first, not pitch-first.
- Use the audit to route visitors toward proof, project paths, or a call based on what they reveal.

## What the audit should help a buyer answer

- Where is the growth system actually broken?
- Is the problem structural, executional, or measurement-related?
- What kind of project path probably fits next?
- What proof should I look at next?

## Relationship to tools

Not every tool needs to remain an equal top-level offer.

Default bias:

- fold overlapping diagnostic logic into the Growth System Audit
- keep standalone tools only when they serve a clear strategic role that the audit cannot replace cleanly

## Relationship to proof and services

The audit should route into:

- similar proof when the visitor needs evidence
- buyer-facing project paths when the likely implementation shape is clear
- a diagnostic call when trust and urgency are high

## CTA constraints

- Avoid stacking multiple low-trust offers on the same surface.
- Avoid mixing equal-weight tool CTAs in the same block.
- Do not let service or call CTAs overwhelm the audit on low-trust pages.
